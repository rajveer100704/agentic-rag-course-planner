================================================================================
SECTION: B.S. in Computer Science — Overview
================================================================================
Total Credits Required: 120 credit hours
Minimum GPA: 2.0 cumulative GPA; 2.0 CS-prefix GPA required to graduate.
Residency Requirement: At least 30 of the final 36 credits must be completed at
State University. Transfer credits may count toward requirements but must be
approved by the CS Advising Office.
Catalog Year Policy: Students follow the degree requirements of the catalog year
in which they first enrolled in a CS course (or declared the major, whichever
is earlier). Students may petition to switch to a newer catalog year.

================================================================================
SECTION: B.S. Computer Science — Core Requirements (Required for ALL students)
================================================================================
The following courses are REQUIRED. No substitutions without written petition
approved by the Department Chair.

FOUNDATION COURSES (13 credits):
  • CS 101 — Introduction to Programming (3 cr)
  • CS 150 — Discrete Mathematics for Computer Science (3 cr)
  • CS 201 — Object-Oriented Programming (3 cr)
  • CS 210 — Computer Systems and Organization (3 cr)
  • MATH 120 — Calculus I (4 cr)  [Note: MATH 121 may substitute with advisor approval]

INTERMEDIATE CORE (16 credits):
  • CS 250 — Data Structures and Algorithms (4 cr)
  • CS 310 — Database Systems (3 cr)
  • CS 311 — Operating Systems (3 cr)
  • CS 301 — Software Engineering (3 cr)
  • CS 390 — Ethics and Society in Computing (3 cr)  [Ethics requirement]

ADVANCED CORE (6 credits — choose ONE of the following tracks):

  Track A: Systems Track
    • CS 411 — Distributed Systems (3 cr)  [requires CS 311 + CS 340]
    • CS 340 — Computer Networks (3 cr)

  Track B: Intelligent Systems Track
    • CS 320 — Machine Learning (3 cr)  [requires CS 250 + CS 260 + CS 220]
    • CS 260 — Linear Algebra for Machine Learning (3 cr)

  Track C: Theory Track
    • CS 330 — Algorithms and Complexity (3 cr)
    • CS 350 — Programming Languages (3 cr)

CAPSTONE (3 credits):
  • CS 490 — Senior Capstone Project (3 cr)

================================================================================
SECTION: B.S. Computer Science — Technical Electives
================================================================================
Students must complete 15 credits of Technical Electives from the approved list.
Rules:
  1. At least 9 of the 15 elective credits must be at the 300-level or above.
  2. At most 6 credits may be from outside the CS department (cross-listed or
     partner-department courses), subject to advisor approval.
  3. Courses used to satisfy the Advanced Core track may NOT also count as
     Technical Electives.
  4. DS 301 and DS 401 are approved Technical Electives for CS majors.

Approved Technical Elective List (CS courses):
  CS 230, CS 240, CS 320, CS 330, CS 340, CS 350, CS 360, CS 370, CS 380,
  CS 410, CS 411, CS 420, CS 430, CS 440, CS 450, CS 398 (up to 3 cr)

================================================================================
SECTION: B.S. Computer Science — General Education Requirements
================================================================================
Students must satisfy State University's General Education (Gen Ed) requirements:
  • English Composition I & II (6 cr) — ENGL 101 and ENGL 102
  • Quantitative Reasoning (satisfied by MATH 120 or higher)
  • Natural Sciences (6 cr, with lab) — 2 lab science courses
  • Social Sciences (6 cr) — 2 approved social science courses
  • Humanities (6 cr) — 2 approved humanities courses
  • Ethics (satisfied by CS 390)
  • Diversity and Inclusion (3 cr) — 1 approved D&I course

Note: CS 390 simultaneously satisfies both the CS Ethics Core requirement and the
University Ethics Gen Ed requirement. It does NOT double-count toward electives.

================================================================================
SECTION: B.S. Computer Science — Free Electives
================================================================================
Students need sufficient free elective credits to reach 120 total.
Typically 12–18 free elective credits are needed depending on AP/transfer credits.
Any course offered at State University may be used as a free elective.

================================================================================
SECTION: B.S. Computer Science — Academic Standing Rules
================================================================================
  • Students must maintain a 2.0 cumulative GPA and 2.0 CS GPA.
  • A student receiving a D or F in a required CS course must retake that course.
  • Required courses may be repeated at most TWICE (three attempts total).
  • After three failed attempts, the student must petition for a fourth attempt
    or may be academically dismissed from the major.
  • Students on academic probation may not enroll in more than 12 credits per term.

================================================================================
SECTION: B.S. Computer Science — Advising Notes
================================================================================
  • Students should aim to complete all Foundation courses by end of sophomore year.
  • The recommended sequence for the Intelligent Systems track:
      Year 1: CS 101, CS 150, MATH 120
      Year 2: CS 201, CS 210, CS 250, CS 220, CS 260
      Year 3: CS 310, CS 311, CS 301, CS 320, CS 390
      Year 4: CS 420 or CS 430, CS 490, Technical Electives
  • CS 150 and MATH 120 may be taken concurrently.
