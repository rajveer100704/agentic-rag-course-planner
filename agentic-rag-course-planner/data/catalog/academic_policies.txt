SECTION: Grading Scale and Grade Points
================================================================================
State University uses the following grading scale:

  A   = 4.0 grade points   (93–100%)
  A-  = 3.7 grade points   (90–92%)
  B+  = 3.3 grade points   (87–89%)
  B   = 3.0 grade points   (83–86%)
  B-  = 2.7 grade points   (80–82%)
  C+  = 2.3 grade points   (77–79%)
  C   = 2.0 grade points   (73–76%)
  C-  = 1.7 grade points   (70–72%)
  D+  = 1.3 grade points   (67–69%)
  D   = 1.0 grade points   (63–66%)
  D-  = 0.7 grade points   (60–62%)
  F   = 0.0 grade points   (below 60%)

  W   = Withdrawal (no grade points; does not affect GPA; counts as an attempt)
  I   = Incomplete (temporary; must be resolved within 6 weeks of term end)
  P/F = Pass/Fail (available for free electives only; not allowed for major
        requirements or Gen Ed requirements without written petition)
  AU  = Audit (no credit; no grade points)

IMPORTANT: A grade of C- is NOT considered a "C or higher." When a course
requirement states "C or higher," the student must earn exactly a C (2.0) or
above. A C- (1.7) does NOT satisfy this requirement.

================================================================================
SECTION: Minimum Grade Requirements for Prerequisites
================================================================================
When a course description states a prerequisite requires a grade of "C or higher,"
this means a letter grade of C or better (A, A-, B+, B, B-, C+ or C).
  • C- does NOT satisfy a "C or higher" prerequisite requirement.
  • A Pass (P) grade satisfies a prerequisite requirement only if the prerequisite
    course was completed under P/F grading AND the department chair provides
    written approval.
  • An Incomplete (I) grade does NOT satisfy a prerequisite. The student must
    resolve the Incomplete and receive a letter grade before the prerequisite
    is considered met.
  • Transfer course equivalencies: Transfer students must have prerequisites
    evaluated by the CS Advising Office. Transfer credit does not automatically
    satisfy prerequisites; an equivalency evaluation is required.

================================================================================
SECTION: Course Repeat Policy
================================================================================
  1. Students may repeat any course in which they received a D+, D, D-, F, or W.
  2. Students who receive a C- in a required course and need a C or higher must
     also repeat the course.
  3. When a course is repeated, BOTH the original and repeated grade appear on
     the transcript. The HIGHER grade is used in GPA calculation (grade
     replacement policy — effective for courses taken after Fall 2020).
     Note: For courses taken before Fall 2020, both grades are averaged.
  4. Required courses may be attempted at most THREE times total. A fourth
     attempt requires a petition to the Academic Standards Committee.
  5. Elective courses may be repeated once. Additional repeats require advisor
     approval.
  6. Students may not repeat a course in which they earned a B- or higher,
     unless they receive written permission from the instructor and department chair.

================================================================================
SECTION: Credit Load and Overload Policy
================================================================================
Standard Credit Load:
  • Full-time: 12–18 credits per semester.
  • Part-time: fewer than 12 credits per semester.
  • The standard maximum is 18 credits per semester without special approval.

Overload Policy (more than 18 credits):
  • Students wishing to enroll in 19–21 credits must have a cumulative GPA of
    3.0 or higher and receive advisor approval.
  • Enrollment in more than 21 credits in a single semester is NOT permitted
    under any circumstances.
  • Summer sessions: maximum 12 credits total across all summer sessions combined.

Academic Probation Credit Limit:
  • Students on academic probation may enroll in a maximum of 12 credits per term.

================================================================================
SECTION: Withdrawal Policy
================================================================================
  • Students may withdraw from a course without academic penalty through the end
    of Week 10 of a 16-week semester (proportional for shorter terms).
  • After Week 10, withdrawal requires dean's approval and will result in a W
    on the transcript regardless.
  • A W counts as an attempt toward the course repeat limit.
  • Withdrawing from a course when it is a co-requisite for another enrolled
    course automatically triggers withdrawal from the co-requisite course as well.

================================================================================
SECTION: Co-requisite Policy
================================================================================
Co-requisites are courses that must be taken IN THE SAME SEMESTER unless the
catalog entry explicitly states "may have been completed in a prior term."
  • If a course lists a co-requisite, students must be concurrently enrolled in
    the co-requisite (or have already completed it with a C or higher) at the
    time of enrollment.
  • Dropping a co-requisite during the term triggers an administrative withdrawal
    from the associated course unless a petition is approved by the department.

================================================================================
SECTION: Instructor Consent / Permission Courses
================================================================================
Some courses require instructor consent (listed as "instructor consent required"
in the course description). For these courses:
  • The student must obtain a Permission to Enroll (PTE) number from the
    instructor or department office.
  • Instructor consent does NOT waive other prerequisites unless the instructor
    explicitly states so in writing.
  • PTE numbers expire at the end of the enrollment period in which they were issued.

================================================================================
SECTION: Transfer Credit Policy
================================================================================
  • Transfer credits are evaluated by the Office of Transfer Admissions and
    the relevant academic department.
  • Transfer credits may satisfy prerequisites only after formal equivalency
    approval from the CS Advising Office.
  • Students transferring with an Associate's degree from an accredited institution
    may receive up to 60 transfer credits.
  • Courses transferred as "general elective credit" (not mapped to a specific
    State University course) do NOT satisfy specific course prerequisites.

================================================================================
SECTION: Academic Probation and Dismissal
================================================================================
  • Academic probation is triggered when the cumulative GPA falls below 2.0 for
    two consecutive terms.
  • Students on academic probation are limited to 12 credit hours per term.
  • Continued GPA below 2.0 for a third consecutive term may result in academic
    dismissal from the major or the university.
  • Dismissed students may apply for reinstatement after one full year away.

================================================================================
SECTION: Graduation Application Deadlines
================================================================================
  • Students must file a graduation application with the Registrar by:
      — October 1 for December graduation
      — March 1 for May graduation
      — June 1 for August graduation
  • Filing late requires a $75 late fee and may delay diploma processing.
  • Degree audits are the responsibility of the student; the Registrar and
    advisor serve in an advisory capacity only.

================================================================================
SECTION: Enrollment Timing and Add/Drop
================================================================================
  • Priority registration is offered to students based on earned credit hours
    (seniors first, then juniors, etc.).
  • The Add/Drop period lasts through the end of Week 2 of a 16-week semester.
  • After Week 2, adding a course requires instructor and dean approval.
  • A course dropped during the Add/Drop period does NOT appear on the transcript.
