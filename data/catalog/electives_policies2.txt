SECTION: Registration FAQ — Prerequisite Overrides
================================================================================
Q: Can a professor override a prerequisite for me?
A: Yes, instructors may grant prerequisite overrides on a case-by-case basis.
The instructor must submit a signed Override Authorization Form to the Registrar.
Prerequisite overrides do not guarantee success in the course; students who
receive overrides are responsible for acquiring any missed foundational knowledge.
Overrides are typically granted in cases where a student has equivalent knowledge
from work experience or non-equivalent transfer courses.

================================================================================
SECTION: Registration FAQ — Waitlists
================================================================================
Q: How do course waitlists work?
A: When a course reaches capacity, eligible students may add themselves to a
waitlist. Waitlist position is determined by the student's registration priority
(based on credit hours). Waitlisted students are auto-enrolled if a seat opens
before the end of Week 1. After Week 1, students on the waitlist must manually
enroll if notified of an opening.

================================================================================
SECTION: Registration FAQ — Concurrent Enrollment at Another Institution
================================================================================
Q: Can I take a course at a community college concurrently while enrolled at
State University?
A: Yes, with prior advisor approval. Credits must be pre-approved via a
Concurrent Enrollment Agreement form. Grades from the outside institution
do not affect State University GPA but may affect financial aid satisfactory
academic progress calculations.
